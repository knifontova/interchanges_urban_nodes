About the research

Research description

The research investigates the interchanges between public transport stops in two European cities, Palermo and Helsinki. For the purpose of this study, an interchange is defined as a connection between two stops, where the routes serviced by these stops do not completely coincide.

Two main indicators were calculated:

  • the number of interchanges from one stop to other stops around
  • length of connections between interchanges stops

We focused only on bus and tram stops to narrow the scope and make the research more manageable. Stops were connected if they fell within a radius of 500 m (5-6 minutes of pedestrian travel) and had at least 1 different route.

The indicators were calculated using GTFS data, a standard for public transportation schedules, and analyzed with QGIS.

Data sources

  • GTFS data from Open Mobility data
  • Basemap layers from OpenMapTiles styled with Maputnik
  • Population data from City Population - Population Statistics in Maps and Charts for Cities, Agglomerations and Administrative Divisions of all Countries of the World
  • Bryniarska, Z., & Zakowska, L. (2017). Multi-criteria evaluation of public transport interchanges. Transportation Research Procedia, 24, 25-32.
