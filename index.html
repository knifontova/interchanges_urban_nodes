About the research

More than 70% of the European Union population lives in urban areas, and this rate is expected to rise to 86% by 2050. It is then necessary to optimize public transport networks to make European cities more resilient to a growing population.

Public transport (PT) interchanges are critical components of urban networks, making trips through cities comfortable or otherwise (Bryniarska & Zakowska, 2017). In urban networks, they can be considered as nodes; large distances between neighboring PT stops make nodes disconnected and routes through the network more complex.

In our analysis, we consider and evaluate the current transport network of bus and tram stops in two European cities. The cities that are under evaluation are Palermo, Italy, and Helsinki, Finland. The choice of these two cities is based on the similar population size and the comparison between an insufficient (Palermo) and a very well-structured (Helsinki) transport system. The goal is to visualize the differences in the current state.
